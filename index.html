Practice Questions

10 scenario-based questions. These are original and targeted to test core RBT knowledge.

Q1
During a baseline observation, a client engages in 10 instances of hitting per hour. After an intervention, the client engages in 2 instances per hour. What is the most appropriate way to report change?
Select the best single answer.
Answer: B.

Use percent reduction to convey relative change when baseline rates differ across clients or conditions. An 80% reduction communicates intervention effectiveness better than raw counts alone. Include baseline and post-intervention rates too for clarity (e.g., baseline 10/hr → 2/hr; 80% reduction).

Q2
A client reliably requests attention by tapping a staff member’s shoulder. You teach a communicative alternative and start reinforcing requests with a 30-second pause before providing attention. Which reinforcement schedule is this?
Answer: C.

A fixed-interval (FI) schedule reinforces the first appropriate response after a fixed amount of time has passed (here, 30 seconds). Fixed-ratio involves number of responses; variable-interval uses unpredictable time; DRO reinforces absence of target behavior rather than the request.

Q3
You are collecting continuous data on a client’s vocal stereotypy. Which measurement system is best for capturing every instance and duration accurately?
Answer: A.

For continuous behaviors with variable duration, continuous event recording plus duration measurement captures both frequency and total time engaged. Interval methods (whole/partial/momentary) estimate occurrence and can under- or over-estimate duration for continuous stereotypy.

Q4
A client shows aggression when denied a preferred item. The team develops a plan to teach tolerance using graduated exposure: brief denials gradually lengthened. Which ethical issue must you monitor closely?
Answer: D.

Graduated exposure involves intentionally exposing the client to brief denial which may cause distress. Ethical practice requires minimizing harm, monitoring client assent/distress, and using the least intrusive effective procedures. Document responses and obtain informed consent from guardians as appropriate.

Q5
Which graph type is best for showing changes in a single client’s target behavior across baseline and intervention phases?
Answer: A.

Single-subject research commonly uses line graphs to show level, trend, and variability across phases for one client. Bar charts and pie charts are not suitable for serial, time-ordered data in behavior analysis.

Q6
During discrete trial training (DTT), you notice the client has high latency to respond. Which modification is best to reduce latency?
Answer: B.

Prompts (modeling, gestural) reduce latency by providing additional stimulus control; then fade prompts systematically to transfer control to the natural discriminative stimulus. Increasing intertrial intervals often increases latency; extinction and removing reinforcement are inappropriate here.

Q7
You collect data using 10-second partial-interval recording and observe high rates for a behavior that is brief and frequent. What is a likely measurement bias?
Answer: D.

Partial-interval recording scores an interval as positive if the behavior occurred at any time during the interval, which can overestimate the true rate for brief, frequent behaviors. Continuous recording is preferred for accurate frequency/duration.

Q8
A behavior support plan includes the strategy: "Provide functional communication training (FCT) for escape-maintained problem behavior." What is the defining feature of FCT?
Answer: A.

FCT teaches a communicative response (e.g., "break please") that is reinforced with the same consequence maintaining problem behavior (escape, attention, tangible). It replaces the function, not punish behavior. DRO may be used alongside but is not FCT itself.

Q9
Which is the correct pairing: partial-interval, whole-interval, momentary time sampling — with the measurement they are best for?
Answer: C.

Partial-interval indicates if behavior occurred during an interval (can overestimate). Whole-interval records if behavior occupied the entire interval (can underestimate). Momentary sampling records whether behavior occurs at an instant—useful for prevalence estimates but not perfect for frequency/duration.

Q10
When implementing token economy, what is critical to ensure tokens function as conditioned reinforcers?
Answer: B.

For tokens to be conditioned reinforcers, they must be paired with meaningful backup reinforcers and consistently exchangeable. Reliability and clear contingency make tokens effective; inconsistency undermines their reinforcing value.

Frequently Asked Questions — RBT Exam & Practice

What should I bring to the RBT exam?
You can bring a government-issued photo ID and your exam appointment confirmation. Follow the test center rules for electronics and breaks.
How long is the RBT exam?
The RBT exam typically allows 90 minutes for 75 questions, but confirm current exam rules from your test administrator.
Can I use practice tests to pass the RBT exam?
Yes — well-designed practice tests improve familiarity with item formats and timing. Review explanations for every wrong answer to learn concepts deeply.
Are calculators allowed during the exam?
Check the exam administrator's rules. Many testing centers allow basic calculators, but policies change—verify ahead of exam day.
How should I track progress while studying?
Track accuracy and timing across practice sets, review wrong items, and monitor trends (accuracy over weeks). Focus on weak domains until improvement is consistent.
What content areas does the RBT test cover?
Core areas include measurement and data, assessment, skill acquisition, behavior reduction, professional conduct, and documentation/communication.
How many practice questions should I do per study session?
Aim for 20–30 quality questions per session with thorough review. Short, frequent sessions beat long, unfocused study marathons.
Should I time myself on practice tests?
Yes — simulate exam conditions occasionally to build pacing. Work on accuracy first, then gradually add timed practice to increase speed.
How do I use incorrect items effectively?
Review why the correct answer is correct and why alternatives are wrong. Re-test yourself on the concept later and create a short note or flashcard for spaced review.
Are these practice questions representative of the actual exam?
These questions are crafted to reflect exam-style scenarios and concepts, but the real exam's exact wording and distribution may differ. Use this as targeted practice and review official resources too.
