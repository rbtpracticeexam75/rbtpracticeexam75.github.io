RBT Professional Conduct Quiz

Click an answer to see if you're correct and view the explanation!

1. What is the primary responsibility of an RBT?
RBTs implement interventions and collect data under the supervision of a BCBA or BCaBA; they do not design programs or diagnose.
2. Which of the following is an example of maintaining professional boundaries?
Maintaining professional boundaries ensures ethical and safe interactions between the RBT and the client.
3. When should an RBT report ethical concerns?
RBTs must report any suspected ethical violations immediately to protect clients and comply with BACB standards.
4. Which of the following behaviors violates professional conduct?
Confidentiality is a core ethical requirement. Sharing private information without consent is strictly prohibited.
5. An RBT receives a gift from a client. What should they do?
Accepting gifts can create conflicts of interest. Always consult the supervising BCBA before accepting anything.
6. What is the correct action if an RBT makes a mistake during data collection?
Transparency and accuracy are crucial in ABA. Errors should be reported immediately to maintain data integrity.
7. Which action aligns with professional conduct regarding supervision?
RBTs must adhere to supervision guidelines, implement programs as directed, and seek clarification when unsure.
8. When is it appropriate for an RBT to end a professional relationship with a client?
Professional termination requires ethical handling, including consulting the BCBA and providing proper notice.
9. Accepting multiple roles (friend, therapist, coach) with the same client is considered:
Dual relationships can lead to conflicts of interest and must be avoided to maintain professional boundaries.
10. How should an RBT handle client confidentiality when discussing cases for supervision?
Client information must remain confidential and only be shared with authorized professionals.
11. Which is an example of professional responsibility?
Accurate and timely documentation is a key professional responsibility for RBTs.
12. Using client data to improve skills without manipulation aligns with:
Ethical practice ensures data is used appropriately to improve client outcomes, without manipulation.
13. How should an RBT respond to a client complaint?
All complaints should be reported to the BCBA to ensure ethical and proper resolution.
14. Misrepresenting your credentials to a client is:
Misrepresentation is a serious ethical violation under the BACB professional code.
15. An RBT is asked to perform a task outside their training. They should:
RBTs must only perform tasks within their competency and report any requests beyond their scope.
