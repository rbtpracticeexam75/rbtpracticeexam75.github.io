Privacy Policy

Effective date: September 18, 2025. This Privacy Policy explains how RBT Practice Exam ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and shares your information when you visit https://rbtpracticeexam75.github.io/ or use our practice tests and resources.

1. What information we collect

We collect only the information necessary to operate the site, improve content, comply with legal obligations, and provide basic interactive features.

  • Automatically collected data: We collect technical data such as IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, referral URL, pages visited, and time spent on pages. This data is collected via server logs and analytics tools.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: We use cookies, local storage, and similar tools to remember preferences, enable features, and analyze traffic. See the Cookies section below for details.
  • Form data: If you choose to contact us (via a contact form or email link), we collect the information you submit (name, email, message) to respond to you.
  • Third-party data: If you interact with third-party services embedded on our site (e.g., YouTube, Google services, affiliate links), those providers may collect data independently. We don't control their practices—see their privacy policies for details.

2. How we use your information

  • To operate and improve the site: Analyze traffic patterns, fix bugs, and make content choices based on aggregated analytics.
  • To respond to messages: Use contact form data to reply to your inquiries and support requests.
  • To provide features: Remember preferences (language, display) and enable interactive features such as practice quizzes.
  • For legal and safety purposes: Comply with legal obligations and protect the site from abuse.

3. Cookies and tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies to improve site functionality and measure usage. Below are common categories:

  • Essential cookies: Required for basic site operation (e.g., session management). These cannot be turned off without affecting site function.
  • Performance and analytics: Used to collect anonymous usage statistics (pageviews, bounce rate). We use Google Analytics / Plausible (depending on configuration) to gather aggregated traffic data.
  • Advertising: If you choose to enable ads (e.g., Google AdSense), advertising cookies may be used by ad networks to serve relevant ads and measure performance. You can opt out via your browser or ad network tools.

Most browsers let you block or delete cookies. To exercise choices, use your browser settings or third-party opt-out tools such as the Google Ad Settings or AdChoices.

Our site may include links or embedded content from third parties, including but not limited to:

These services collect data as described in their own privacy policies. We recommend reviewing their policies if you are concerned about data collection by third parties.

We do not sell personal data. We may share data in the following limited circumstances:

  • With service providers who process data on our behalf (hosting, analytics, email providers).

  • To protect rights and property, respond to claims, or prevent fraud or security issues.
  • In connection with a merger, sale, or reorganization of the site — but only as permitted by law and with appropriate protections.

Depending on where you live, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request a copy of personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: Ask us to correct inaccurate information.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal exceptions).

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the address below. We may ask for information to verify your identity before fulfilling requests.
